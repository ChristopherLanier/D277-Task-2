History of Cleveland

Cleveland, named after the the American Revolutionary veteran General Moses Cleaveland, was said to be in the 1st rank of american manufacturing cities after the civil war and the "best location in the nation" in the late 20th century. Characterized by unprecented growth in the steel and automotive industries, Cleveland was a powerhouse until the 1960's where an economic downturn began to occur. Today Cleveland has a more diversified economy.
